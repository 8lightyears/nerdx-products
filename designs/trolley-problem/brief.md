# Design Brief: Trolley Problem

**Status:** draft
**Theme group:** Philosophy (`themes/philosophy.md` §4)

---

## Idea

The trolley problem (Foot 1967; Thomson 1985) is the world's most recognisable ethics
thought experiment: a runaway trolley is heading toward five people; you can pull a lever
to redirect it onto a side track where one person stands. Pull the lever → five saved, one
killed. Do nothing → five killed, one lives. No right answer — the split is ~73/27 (Awad
et al. 2018, 40M responses, 233 countries).

The product is a **two-shirt set** that works as a social pair or solo:

- Shirt A: lever in its UP position — "Pull the lever." (73%)
- Shirt B: lever in its DOWN position — "Don't pull the lever." (27%)

Shirts mean nothing on their own; next to each other the whole joke lands. That dynamic
creates social media moments without any campaign budget.

---

## Slogans

**Primary (shirt A):** "Pull the lever. (73%)"
**Primary (shirt B):** "Don't pull the lever. (27%)"
**Alternative one-liner (either):** "It's a thought experiment. (You still hesitated.)"
**Stats variant:** "73% pull the lever. Are you sure?" — on the chest; full citation on sleeve or back hem.

---

## Visual direction

**Layout — track schematic (top-down):**
- Two rails run across the shirt, converging at a Y-junction.
- The lever is shown at the junction:
  - Shirt A: lever tilted RIGHT — track diverted to the single person.
  - Shirt B: lever tilted LEFT (default position) — track continues straight to the group.
- Five stylised stick figures at the end of the straight track; one at the end of the
  side track. Minimal, diagrammatic — think technical-drawing aesthetic, not cartoon.
- The lever itself is the focal graphic; bold, clean mechanical line.

**Typography:** clean monospaced or geometric sans (JetBrains Mono SIL OFL 1.1, or IBM
Plex Mono SIL OFL 1.1). Keeps the technical / logical mood.

**Palette options:**
1. Black track + lever on white shirt — clearest, most versatile.
2. Chalk-on-blackboard: cream lines on dark grey/charcoal — academic feel.
3. Blueprint: white lines on denim blue — engineering aesthetic, complements the
   thought-experiment framing.

**Size:** track graphic centred on front, roughly 20×16 cm. Slogan below, small.

**Generation:** 100% original work in Matplotlib or Inkscape — no third-party images,
no copyright exposure. See `generate/track.py`.

---

## Variants and expansion

- **Mug:** single mug, lever schematic, slogan "Which mug are you?" — table conversation piece.
- **Stats poster (A3):** full design with "233 countries. 40 million responses. 73%."
  and a small world heatmap (original, based on published Awad et al. data). Data is
  published academic research — citation, not reproduction.
- **Multi-person set (3-shirt):** add a third shirt for the trolley itself
  ("I'm the trolley.") — absurdist variant.
