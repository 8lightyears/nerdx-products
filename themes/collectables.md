# Theme: Collectable Series — Mugs, Prints, and Sets

## What this family covers

Products that gain value in multiples — a single mug is nice; the full set of eight
planets becomes a conversation piece. Three series proposed here share a common
logic: each object in the set is scientifically distinct, visually differentiated,
and the set as a whole tells a coherent story. All visual material either derives
from US-government imagery (NASA, USGS — PD on publication) or is purpose-built
original artwork requiring no third-party license.

---

## Series 1 — Planets of the Solar System

### Concept

Eight mugs (or prints), one per planet. Each shows a high-quality image of the
planet centred on a dark background, with a small data strip below: diameter, mass
relative to Earth, orbital period, and one memorable fact. The series reads as both
a reference set and a collector's object.

### Imagery

All principal NASA mission images are US government works — public domain on
publication. No license research needed.

| Planet | Primary image source | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mercury | MESSENGER mosaic, 2013 | NASA/JHUAPL/CIW; full-surface map available |
| Venus | Magellan radar map (false colour), 1990–94 | NASA/JPL; cloud-surface composite |
| Earth | Apollo 17 "Blue Marble", 1972 | NASA AS17-148-22727; iconic |
| Mars | Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter colour mosaic | NASA/JPL/USGS; global map available |
| Jupiter | Cassini approach image, 2000 | NASA/JPL/University of Arizona; Great Red Spot centred |
| Saturn | Cassini equinox image, 2009 | NASA/JPL/SSI; rings edge-on; one of the finest images ever |
| Uranus | Voyager 2 true colour, 1986 | NASA/JPL; subtle blue-green |
| Neptune | Voyager 2, 1989 | NASA/JPL; Great Dark Spot; vivid blue |

**Where to obtain:**

- NASA Image and Video Library: https://images.nasa.gov — full-resolution TIFF/JPEG
  downloads, all NASA works PD (NASA has no copyright on its own imagery).
- NASA Photojournal (JPL): https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov — mission-specific
  archives; download at full resolution.
- Planetary.org Image Library: additional processed versions, PD NASA originals.

**T-shirt / mug potential:** Very high. The "complete solar system" angle makes the
set self-selling — customers who buy one planet are likely to return for others.
Saturn is the strongest single-item seller (rings are immediately recognisable);
Earth "Blue Marble" has obvious broad appeal. The data strip differentiates the
product from a poster reprint.

**Production note:** NASA TIFF files for planetary images are typically 2,000–8,000 px
on the long edge — sufficient for a 15 oz mug wrap (approximately 3,000 × 2,000 px
at 200 DPI) without upscaling. Colour profile adjustment from sRGB to print profile
is the main production step.

**Series copy line options:**

- "The Solar System — collected."
- "Eight worlds. One shelf."
- Planet name + data strip only (no tagline) — minimal and scientific.

## Series 2 — Dwarf Planets

### Concept

Five mugs (or prints), one per IAU-recognised dwarf planet. The commercial hook is
the recency and the underdog angle: these are real worlds, scientifically complex,
and almost entirely ignored by popular science products. The Pluto entry capitalises
on the "demoted planet" cultural moment.

| Dwarf planet | Status | Primary mission | Image source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ceres | Inner solar system; volcanic ice | Dawn spacecraft, 2015–18 | NASA/JPL-Caltech/UCLA/MPS/DLR/IDA — PD |
| Pluto | Kuiper belt; nitrogen glaciers, mountain ranges | New Horizons, 2015 | NASA/JHUAPL/SwRI — PD |
| Eris | Scattered disc; brighter than Pluto | No close flyby; Hubble only | HST image PD; lower resolution |
| Haumea | Egg-shaped; fastest-rotating large body in solar system | No close flyby | Artistic impression; no close-approach image |
| Makemake | Kuiper belt | No close flyby | Hubble; low resolution |

**Where to obtain:**

- Dawn images: https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/mission/Dawn — full resolution, PD
- New Horizons images: https://www.nasa.gov/mission/new-horizons/ — PD
- Hubble images: https://hubblesite.org/images — PD (STScI/NASA)

**Design note for Eris, Haumea, Makemake:** No close-approach spacecraft images
exist. Options: (a) use Hubble resolved images with honest caption ("as seen from
Earth"); (b) commission original scientific illustrations modelled on published size
and albedo data — these are facts, not creative expression, so no license concern.

**T-shirt / mug potential:** High for Pluto specifically; medium for the others on
their own. As a complete set, the "forgotten five" angle is strong. Pluto works well
as a standalone shirt: "Still interesting. Always will be." + New Horizons heart
feature (Tombaugh Regio) image.

**Series copy line options:**

- "The worlds they didn't teach you in school."
- "Demoted, not forgotten." (Pluto-specific; works as a series tagline too)
- Dwarf planet name + discovery date + one physical fact.

## Series 3 — Geological Eras of Earth

### Concept

A set of prints or mugs representing the major divisions of Earth's 4.5 billion-year
history, each centred on the most visually striking life form or geological event of
that interval. The series can be sold as: (a) four eons (Hadean–Archean–Proterozoic–
Phanerozoic); (b) twelve periods of the Phanerozoic; or (c) a hybrid of six or eight
"chapters" that balance visual variety with set size. Recommended: eight chapters
(Hadean through Quaternary), curated for visual contrast.

### Proposed eight-chapter set

| Chapter | Time | Signature image | PD source notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hadean | 4.5–4.0 Ga | Magma ocean; early Moon impact cataclysm | Original digital painting; no PD image exists — too deep in time |
| Archean | 4.0–2.5 Ga | Stromatolites — oldest fossil life | Modern stromatolites (Shark Bay, Australian gov image); or Gunflint Chert 1954 microphotos — check NRC Canada rights |
| Proterozoic | 2.5–0.54 Ga | Snowball Earth glaciers; early multicellular life | Original diagram; global ice map is PD (USGS/GSA) |
| Cambrian | 541–485 Ma | Anomalocaris; Burgess Shale explosion | Walcott Burgess Shale photographs, Smithsonian 1909–11 — PD (Smithsonian pre-1928 works); Haeckel-era illustrations not specific to Cambrian but style is usable |
| Carboniferous–Permian | 359–252 Ma | Coal-forest canopy; first amniotes | Reconstruction illustrations from Niklas 1992 and older sources; pre-1928 Charles Knight paintings of Carboniferous in public domain where published before 1927 |
| Triassic–Jurassic | 252–145 Ma | Dinosaur fauna; early mammals in the margins | Marsh & Cope bone-war era anatomical plates 1877–1893 — PD (USGS Geological Survey publications) |
| Cretaceous | 145–66 Ma | Tyrannosaurus; mosasaurs; K-Pg impact | Marsh 1890 *Dinosaurs of North America* plates — PD; or Haeckel 1904 Kunstformen der Natur for marine fauna (PD) |
| Cenozoic (Paleogene–Quaternary) | 66–0 Ma | Mammals radiate; hominins emerge | Joseph Smit illustrations (Lydekker *Royal Natural History* 1893–96) — PD pre-1928 |

**PD sources for this series:**

- USGS geological time scale and diagrams: https://www.usgs.gov/media/images/
  geologic-time-scale — US government work, PD.
- Smithsonian Institution Research Online: Walcott's Burgess Shale plates
  (published in Smithsonian Miscellaneous Collections, 1909–1912) — PD.
- Marsh's US Geological Survey publications (1877, 1883, 1890, 1893):
  https://pubs.usgs.gov/gip/dinosaurs/ — US government work, PD.
- Haeckel, *Kunstformen der Natur* (1904): Internet Archive,
  https://archive.org/details/kunstformenderna00haec — PD pre-1928; 100
  lithographic plates of marine creatures, radiolarians, jellyfish, sea anemones —
  directly usable for Precambrian and Paleozoic marine life entries.
- Joseph Smit illustrations in *Royal Natural History* (1893–96): Internet Archive,
  https://archive.org/details/royalnaturalhist01lydk — PD.

**T-shirt / mug potential:** High for the complete series; medium for individual
pieces without context. The geological timeline angle is strong for science audiences:
every geologist, palaeontologist, and earth-science student will recognise the series
concept immediately. The Cambrian explosion chapter (Anomalocaris, Opabinia) is the
strongest standalone visual.

**Series copy line options:**

- "Earth — all previous versions."
- "4.5 billion years. 8 chapters."
- Era name + time span + one line of context (e.g. "Cambrian — 541 Ma —
  the moment complex life learned to see").

**Production note for pre-1928 illustrations:** Marsh USGS plates and Walcott Smithsonian
plates are high-resolution lithographs; scan quality on Internet Archive varies by issue.
USGS published companion high-resolution scans at pubs.usgs.gov. For the Hadean and
Proterozoic chapters, original digital artwork is the only practical option — no PD
imagery from that era of Earth can exist. Commission scope: 2 original pieces per set
if using the eight-chapter structure.

## Visual direction

- **Consistent format across all three series:** identical layout template — image
  centred, dark background, series name in a small corner badge, factual data strip
  at the bottom in a light serif. The template makes the set visually coherent without
  making each piece identical.
- **Mug wrap vs. print:** The planet and dwarf-planet images are naturally circular
  (disk images) and look good centred on a mug. The geological-eras series is better
  suited to flat prints or tall mugs (15 oz / 20 oz) where a tall image works.
- **Dark background default:** all NASA planetary images already have black
  backgrounds; maintaining that is zero additional work.

## Production workflow

1. Download NASA TIFF from Photojournal at full resolution.
2. Resize to target product dimensions (mug wrap or print size).
3. Overlay data strip (original typography — no third-party fonts required beyond
   a system serif).
4. Export at 200–300 DPI depending on product.
5. Geological eras: assemble PD plate scans via ImageMagick pipeline (same as
   public-domain-space.md automation notes); commission 2 original pieces for
   Hadean and Proterozoic.
