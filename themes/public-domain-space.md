# Theme: Public-Domain Space, Sci-Fi & Forgotten Beliefs

## What this family covers

Pre-1927 speculative and astronomical imagery: Victorian and Edwardian illustrations of
other worlds, astronomical drawings, cosmological diagrams, and early sci-fi concepts that
are now definitively in the US public domain. Every item listed below has been individually
assessed; the PD basis is stated alongside each entry.

This theme fits the shop's spirit precisely: the images are obscure, factually interesting,
and start conversations. A shirt showing Wells's Martian creatures from 1908 is simultaneously
art history, science history, and a visual punchline.

## Sub-ideas and catalogued assets

### 1. H.G. Wells / W.R. Leigh — "The Things That Live on Mars" (Cosmopolitan, 1908)

**PD basis:** Published March 1908 in Cosmopolitan (Vol. 44, No. 5), a US periodical.
Under US copyright law, works published before 1 January 1928 are in the public domain.
No copyright renewal is possible for a 1908 work. The Library of Congress has digitised
this item (cosmos000114), which confirms their determination that it is unrestricted.

**What it contains:**

H.G. Wells wrote a nine-page speculative essay about Martian life, biology, and civilisation.
William Robinson Leigh — an American painter best known for Western landscapes — produced
four full-page illustrations in collaboration with Wells, depicting:

1. **Martian Flora** — alien plant life adapted to a thin, cold, CO₂-rich atmosphere.
   Low gravity means vast canopy spreads; sparse chlorophyll.
2. **Martian Fauna** — land creatures; Wells argued low gravity would favour large, lightly
   built bodies. Leigh depicted elongated limbs and thin torsos.
3. **The Ruling Inhabitants** — speculative humanoid Martians, gaunt and large-headed;
   high intelligence implied by the disproportionate cranium (classic trope, here its origin).
4. **Martian Civilisation** — architecture and cityscape adapted to thin atmosphere and
   minimal weather; low domes, open plazas, minimal load-bearing structure.

**Where to obtain the scans:**

- Library of Congress: https://www.loc.gov/item/cosmos000114
  (item no. cosmos000114; downloadable as high-resolution TIFF)
- Internet Archive PDF of the original magazine pages:
  https://epizodyspace.ru/bibl/inostr-yazyki/cosmopolitan/1908/4/Wells_The_Things_that_Live_on_Mars.pdf
  (checked 2026-06-23)

**T-shirt potential:** Very high. The "Ruling Inhabitants" illustration is the most striking —
gaunt, serious-looking beings with large heads. Caption option: "The Ruling Inhabitants, 1908"
with the source attribution. The alien-landscape images are atmospheric backgrounds.

**Automation:** No automation needed. Download the TIFF from LOC, clean up as needed in GIMP or
Photoshop (remove halftone dots, adjust contrast), and export as print-ready PNG. Manual work:
~2 hours per plate.

### 2. Étienne Léopold Trouvelot — Astronomical Drawings (1882)

**PD basis:** Published 1882, well before the 1928 cutoff. NYPL has confirmed public domain
status and released all 15 drawings under CC0 (no rights reserved).

**What it contains:**

Trouvelot was a French-American astronomer who created over 7,000 astronomical illustrations.
The 1882 atlas contains 15 large-format pastel drawings of extraordinary quality:

| Subject | Notes |
|---|---|
| Aurora Borealis | Sweeping curtains of light; dramatic colour — directly usable |
| The Great Comet of 1881 | Tail spanning the sky; dark background, bright nucleus |
| Total Eclipse of the Sun (1878) | Corona detail; the finest pre-photographic eclipse image |
| The Moon (full disc) | Crater detail at resolution competitive with early photography |
| Jupiter | Banded atmosphere, Red Spot; vivid colours |
| Saturn | Rings at angle; parchment-quality illustration |
| Mars | Surface markings; 1870s understanding of "canals" implied |
| Solar Protuberances | Arcs of plasma in cross-section |
| The Milky Way (central region) | Star cloud rendering |
| Gegenschein and Zodiacal Light | Subtle nocturnal phenomena |

**Where to obtain:**

- NYPL Digital Collections: https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/collections/the-trouvelot-astronomical-drawings-atlas
  (high-resolution TIFF downloads, CC0, free)
- Internet Archive mirror: https://archive.org/details/TrouvelotAstronomicalDrawings
- Rawpixel curated collection: https://www.rawpixel.com/board/281166/trouvelots-astronomy-free-public-domain-astronomical-drawings
  (checked 2026-06-23)

**T-shirt potential:** Very high. Aurora Borealis is an obvious choice — colourful, dramatic,
and already prints-ready. The Great Comet and Total Eclipse are striking without being
predictable. These images are large enough to print without upscaling: NYPL TIFFs are 72 MB+.

**Automation:** None needed. NYPL files are ready for direct use after minimal white-to-transparent
conversion (the pastels have cream backgrounds; colour-select and erase, or use a dark-themed
compositing in Photoshop). Manual work: ~1 hour per image.

### 3. The Flammarion Engraving (ca. 1888)

**PD basis:** First published in Camille Flammarion's *L'atmosphère: météorologie populaire*
(Paris: Hachette, 1888). Anonymous artist. 1888 is definitively pre-1928; Wikimedia Commons
hosts it as PD.

**What it is:**

A wood engraving showing a medieval pilgrim kneeling at the edge of the flat-Earth firmament,
pushing his head through to observe the cosmos beyond. One of the most widely recognised
images in the history of cosmology — it captures humanity's urge to look past the boundary.
Caption in the original: "A medieval missionary tells that he has found the point where
heaven and Earth meet…"

**Where to obtain:**

- Wikimedia Commons: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Flammarion.jpg
  (black-and-white original; also a hand-coloured version)
- PICRYL high-resolution: https://picryl.com/media/flammarion-woodcut-1888-color-1-5b134b
  (checked 2026-06-23)

**T-shirt potential:** High for the right audience. The image is famous enough that physicists
and astronomers will recognise it instantly; obscure enough that non-specialists ask about it.
Excellent conversation-starter shirt. Works best on white or cream shirts (the original is
a black-on-white engraving). Possible caption: "Looking past the edge — 1888."

**Automation:** None needed. Download from Wikimedia or PICRYL. The line engraving is
vector-friendly: trace in Inkscape for a clean SVG.

### 4. LOC Astronomy Archive — broad secondary pool

**PD basis:** The Library of Congress hosts 15,000+ astronomy images; items pre-dating 1928 are
confirmed public domain by LOC archivists before digitisation.

**Where to browse:**

- LOC Astronomy Archive: https://loc.getarchive.net/topics/astronomy
  (checked 2026-06-23)
- Public Domain Review — Astronomy & Space collection:
  https://publicdomainreview.org/collections/images/theme/astronomy-and-space/
  (curated highlights with provenance)

**Highlights worth investigating in this pool:**

- Planetary diagrams from Flammarion's *Astronomie Populaire* (1880) — 300+ engravings.
- James Nasmyth & James Carpenter's moon illustrations (1874) — constructed plaster models
  photographed to simulate lunar surface; hauntingly realistic for 1874.
- Celestial atlas plates from Urania's Mirror (1824) — constellations as figure engravings;
  each on its own card with star holes for backlit viewing.

## Visual direction

- **Dark shirt default:** these images were made before full-colour printing. Inverting their
  tonal scheme (dark background, light detail) works naturally — the Trouvelot pastels already
  have dark backgrounds; the engravings invert cleanly.
- **Minimal text:** the image speaks; add only a date and source as a caption in a small serif.
- **Series potential:** Leigh's four Mars plates form a natural four-shirt or four-mug series.
  The Trouvelot 15 form a collector series. The Flammarion stands alone.

## Automation notes

No generative AI is needed for this theme. The images are already made:
- Tonal cleanup: GIMP Script-Fu or ImageMagick batch command for halftone removal.
- White-to-transparent: `convert input.png -white-threshold 90% -transparent white output.png`
  (ImageMagick); check threshold against each original.
- Upscaling if needed: Real-ESRGAN or Waifu2x for line art; Gigapixel AI for pastels.
